# Using the Zawly Shared Calendar

The **shared family calendar** is the heart of Zawly. It shows everyone in your family what is happening, from school pickups to work trips, all in one place that updates instantly for every member.

## Why a Shared Calendar Changes Everything

Before using a **shared calendar app**, most families rely on:
- Text messages: "Don't forget Sam has football at 4"
- Sticky notes: constantly falling off the fridge
- Memory: "Did I tell you about the dentist appointment?"

With Zawly, there's **one source of truth**. Add it once, everyone sees it.

## Navigating the Calendar

The calendar interface is designed to be intuitive for all ages:

### Month Navigation
- Use the **month selector** at the top to jump to any month (past or future)
- Click the **left/right arrows** to move backward or forward one month
- Today's date is always highlighted for quick orientation

### Viewing Events
- **Click on any date** to see all events for that day
- Events show the title, time, and any notes you added
- Click on an event itself to view full details or make changes

## Adding Events

Adding to your **family calendar** takes seconds:

1. Click on any date in the calendar
2. Fill in the event details:
   - **Title**: Be specific — "School Pickup — 3:15 PM" not just "School"
   - **Start time** and **End time**: Helps family members plan around it
   - **All Day**: Toggle on for holidays, full-day events
   - **Notes**: Add useful details like "Bring swimming kit" or "Conference Room B"
3. Set **Recurrence** if it happens regularly (see our [recurring events guide](/blog/recurring-events-made-simple))
4. Click **Save** — everyone in your family sees it immediately

## Editing and Deleting Events

Things change. Here's how to handle it:

### Editing an Event
1. Click on the event
2. Choose **Edit**
3. Make your changes (time, title, notes, recurrence)
4. Click **Save** — updates for everyone instantly

### Deleting an Event
1. Click on the event
2. Choose **Delete**
3. Choose **Delete This Occurrence** (for recurring events) or **Delete Series**
4. Confirm — it's removed from everyone's calendar

## Holiday Integration

One of Zawly's most loved features is **public holiday integration**:

- When setting up your family, select your country
- Bank holidays, school holidays, and public holidays appear automatically
- Holidays show in a distinct style so you can tell them apart
- Helpful for planning around school breaks and national holidays

### Modifying Holiday Display
Holidays are automatically added based on your family's country setting. They appear as **all-day events** and can't be edited (they're fixed dates), but they help you:
- Avoid booking appointments on bank holidays
- Plan family trips during school breaks
- Know when shops and services might be closed

## Colour-Coding and Visual Organisation

While Zawly keeps things simple, events are easy to scan:
- **Time-based**: Morning, afternoon, and evening events are listed chronologically
- **Recurring events**: Marked with a repeat indicator
- **All-day events**: Span the full date for easy spotting

## Keeping Everyone in Sync

Because the calendar is **truly shared**:

- No need to send calendar invites or forward emails
- Add it once and every family member sees it
- Changes appear instantly — no sync delays
- Works on phones, tablets, and computers

## Calendar Best Practices

### 1. Be Specific with Titles
- ❌ "School"
- ✅ "School Drop-off 8:15 AM — Main Gate"

### 2. Use Notes for Extra Context
- "Bring signed permission slip"
- "Pick up Sam too — he's staying for tea"
- "Remember to pack football kit"

### 3. Check the Calendar Daily
Make it a habit (especially for kids):
- Check tonight for tomorrow's events
- Look at the week ahead every Sunday
- Scan the month for upcoming holidays or busy periods

### 4. Don't Overload the Calendar
Not everything needs to be an event:
- Daily routines (brushing teeth) — these are life, not calendar items
- One-off reminders — use your phone's reminder app
- Things that don't involve multiple people — keep it for shared family life

## Common Calendar Questions

**"My event isn't showing for other family members"**
- Make sure they've accepted their invitation and are part of the family
- Refresh their calendar page
- Check you clicked "Save" after creating the event

**"Can I see past events?"**
- Yes! Use the month selector to go back to any month
- Past events remain visible for reference

**"How do I handle recurring events that change?"**
- Edit the series to update all future occurrences
- Or edit just one occurrence to handle one-off changes

**"Can I print my calendar?"**
- Currently, Zawly is designed for digital use
- You can take screenshots of specific months if needed
- A print/export feature is being considered for future updates

## Combining Calendar with Expenses

Zawly's strength is linking **calendar and finances**:
- Calendar shows *when* things happen
- Expenses show *what they cost*
- Dashboard brings both together

For example:
- "Swimming Lessons" on calendar (Tuesday 4 PM)
- "Swimming Lessons — £40" in expenses (monthly category)
- Dashboard shows both the time commitment and financial impact

## Tips for Different Age Groups

### For Young Children (5-10)
- Show them the calendar night before
- Point out *their* events (school, activities)
- Use simple language: "Swimming tomorrow at 4"

### For Pre-Teens (11-13)
- Encourage them to check it themselves
- Let them add their own events (friends' birthdays, activities)
- Teach them to read times and plan ahead

### For Teenagers (14+)
- They should be fully independent with the calendar
- They can add work shifts, social events, study sessions
- They can edit their own events as things change

### For Adults
- Use it for work schedules, appointments, and social plans
- Coordinate who's doing school runs and pickups
- Plan date nights and adult time too!

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I share my calendar with people outside my family?**
A: Currently, Zawly is designed for private family groups. Only invited family members can see your calendar. For external sharing, you could take screenshots of specific dates.

**Q: What happens if I delete my account?**
A: You can delete your account at any time. Your personal data is removed. Events and expenses you created may remain visible to other family members.

**Q: Can I have multiple families (co-parenting situation)?**
A: Each Zawly account is linked to one family. For co-parenting, you'd need separate accounts for each family workspace.

**Q: Is there a mobile app to download?**
A: Zawly runs in your web browser on any device — phone, tablet, or computer. No download needed, and it works beautifully on mobile.

**Q: Can I set reminders for events?**
A: Currently, Zawly shows events on the calendar but doesn't send reminder notifications. Your device's built-in calendar or alarm can be used alongside Zawly for time-specific reminders.
