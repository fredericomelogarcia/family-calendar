# Planning School Holidays and Activities

School holidays should be fun, not chaotic. With **bank holiday integration** and a shared family calendar, Zawly helps you plan activities that the whole family enjoys — without the stress of last-minute scrambling.

## Why Plan School Holidays in Advance?

Unplanned holidays often lead to:
- **Bored children** moping around the house
- **Overspending** on last-minute entertainment
- **Parental stress** trying to fill every day
- **Clashes** with work commitments or appointments

With Zawly, you can see **holidays automatically marked** on your calendar and plan around them.

## See Holidays Automatically

When you set up your family in Zawly, **public holidays** for your country appear on the calendar automatically:

### What Gets Added Automatically
- **Bank holidays** (when banks and many shops close)
- **School holidays** (term breaks, half-term)
- **Public holidays** (Christmas, Easter, Bonfire Night, etc.)
- **Regional holidays** (specific to your country/region)

### Why This Helps
- Avoid booking **appointments on bank holidays**
- Plan **family trips** during school breaks
- Know when **shops and services** might be closed
- Understand why the calendar looks busy certain weeks

## Planning Activities as a Family

The best holiday plans involve everyone. Here's how to use Zawly:

### Step 1: Check the Calendar Together
1. Go to **Calendar** as a family
2. Look at the upcoming school holiday dates (marked on the calendar)
3. Count how many days you have free
4. Discuss: "What do we want to do with this time?"

### Step 2: Add Activities as Events
For each planned activity:
1. Click on a holiday or free day
2. Add an event like "Trip to the Zoo" or "Movie Afternoon"
3. Set the time (if applicable)
4. Add notes: what to bring, departure time, ticket booking references
5. Save — everyone in the family sees the plan

### Step 3: Involve the Kids
Let children add their own events:
- "Friend's Birthday Party" on Saturday
- "Sleepover at Sam's" next Friday
- "Football Tournament" next month
- "Ice Skating with School" Wednesday

Because everyone can see the calendar, you won't double-book or forget pickups.

## Recurring Activities During Holidays

School holidays often have **recurring activities** that continue regardless:

### Sports and Clubs
- **Weekly**: Swimming lessons (Tuesday and Thursday)
- **Bi-weekly**: Football training (Saturday mornings)
- **Monthly**: Martial arts grading (first Sunday)

### Family Traditions
- **Weekly**: "Pizza and Movie Night" (Fridays)
- **Bi-weekly**: "Sunday Roast at Grandma's"
- **Monthly**: "Family Board Game Night" (last Saturday)

### Setting Up Recurring Holiday Activities
1. Add the activity to the calendar on its first occurrence
2. Set **Recurrence** to match the pattern (Weekly, Bi-weekly, etc.)
3. Add a note: "Bring kit", "Remember water bottle"
4. Save — it appears throughout the holidays automatically

## Balancing Rest and Activity

A good school holiday has a mix of:
- **Planned activities** (trips, clubs, playdates)
- **Free time** (let kids be bored — it sparks creativity!)
- **Family time** (something everyone does together)
- **Parent time** (yes, you need a break too!)

### Using the Dashboard to Check Balance
The **Dashboard** shows you everything coming up:
1. Check if a day is getting **too busy**
2. Spread activities across the week
3. Make sure there's **downtime** too
4. Adjust as needed — the calendar is easy to edit

### Sample Well-Balanced Holiday Week

| Day | Morning | Afternoon | Evening |
|-----|---------|----------|---------|
| Monday | Late breakfast | Cinema trip (added to Zawly) | Family games night |
| Tuesday | Swimming lesson (recurring event) | Free play | Dinner prep together |
| Wednesday | Library visit | Playdate at park | Early night — movie |
| Thursday | Swimming lesson | Baking afternoon | Quiet evening |

## Different Age Groups, Different Needs

### Ages 5-8: Structured but Simple
- **2-3 activities per week** (not every day)
- Lots of **free play time**
- **One big trip** per week (zoo, theme park, etc.)
- **Routine maintenance** (swimming, reading, chores)

### Ages 9-13: Social and Active
- **Playdates and sleepovers** (add them to the calendar!)
- **Hobby clubs** (many run through holidays)
- **Day trips** with friends or family
- **Responsibility** (chores, helping with younger siblings)

### Ages 14+: Independent but Connected
- **Part-time work** (add shifts to calendar)
- **Social events** (parties, trips with friends)
- **Volunteering or work experience**
- **Family time** (still important, even if they protest!)

## Holiday Budgeting with Zawly

School holidays can be expensive. Use **Expenses** to track costs:

### Common Holiday Expenses
| Category | Examples | Typical Cost |
|----------|----------|--------------|
| **Activities** | Cinema, bowling, trampoline park | £10-£30 per child |
| **Days Out** | Zoo, theme park, museum | £40-£100 per family |
| **Food & Treats** | Ice cream, lunches out | £5-£15 per person |
| **Clothing** | New shoes, summer gear | £20-£60 per child |
| **Travel** | Petrol, train tickets, parking | £10-£50 per trip |

### Tracking Holiday Spending
1. Go to **Expenses** and click **Add Transaction**
2. Choose **Expense** and select "Activities" or "Days Out"
3. Enter the amount and what it was for
4. Save — now you know how much the holidays are costing
5. Check the **Dashboard** to see if you're overspending

### Setting a Holiday Budget
Before the holidays start:
1. Look at last year's holiday spending on **Expenses**
2. Set a realistic budget for this year
3. Add it as a note on the first holiday date: "Budget: £300"
4. Track spending weekly — are you on track?

## Common Holiday Planning Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Over-Scheduling
**The Problem**: Every day has an activity, kids (and parents) are exhausted.
**The Fix**: Use the **Dashboard** to check daily. Aim for 60% planned, 40% free time.

### Mistake 2: Last-Minute Planning
**The Problem**: "What are we doing today?" at 9 AM — stress for everyone.
**The Fix**: Spend 30 mins on Sunday planning the week using Zawly's **Calendar**.

### Mistake 3: Forgetting Costs
**The Problem**: "That day out cost HOW much?" at the end of the month.
**The Fix**: Record holiday expenses **immediately** on Zawly's **Expenses** page.

### Mistake 4: Not Involving Kids
**The Problem**: Parents plan everything, kids complain it's boring.
**The Fix**: Let kids add their own events to the **shared calendar**. They'll be more excited about plans they helped create.

## Special Circumstances

### Rainy Day Plans
British weather is unpredictable! Keep a list of **indoor activities** ready:
- Cinema, bowling, trampoline park (add to calendar when needed)
- Museum, library, shopping centre play areas
- Home activities: baking, movies, board games

### Working Parents During Holidays
- Use Zawly to coordinate **childcare** (add as calendar events)
- "Dad's WFH — kids home" (all-day event)
- "Holiday club" (recurring event, specific times)
- "Grandma's day" (one-off or recurring)

### Large Families
- With 3+ kids, holidays get complex fast
- **Colour-code by child** in your mind (not on Zawly, but in your planning)
- "Sam's swimming" vs "Emma's football" — clear notes help!
- The **shared calendar** prevents: "I thought YOU were taking them!"

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How far in advance should I plan school holidays?**
A: Start 2 weeks before for big trips (zoos, theme parks need booking). Add recurring activities 1 week before. Keep some days completely free!

**Q: My kids are bored even with activities planned — what do I do?**
A: They might be over-scheduled! Use the Dashboard to check if you've done too much. Boredom sparks creativity — it's not a bad thing.

**Q: How do I track holiday spending when we're away?**
A: Use your phone! Log into Zawly on any device, go to **Expenses**, and add transactions immediately. Don't let receipts pile up.

**Q: Can I share our holiday plans with grandparents?**
A: Zawly is for family members only. Take a screenshot of the calendar and send it via WhatsApp or email to keep extended family in the loop.

**Q: What if our holiday plans change last minute?**
A: That's life! Click on the event in Zawly's **Calendar** and edit or delete it. Add the new plan. The shared calendar updates instantly for everyone.
